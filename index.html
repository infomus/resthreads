The fine print, up front.

Does it integrate with Yardi / Entrata / RealPage / Knock?

Leads arrive as standard guest cards emailed to each community’s leasing inbox, which every major CRM already ingests. Launch takes a day, not an IT project. Direct API integrations are on the roadmap as we join each vendor’s partner program.

What if a resident says something negative?

Occasionally they will. That honesty is why prospects trust the channel enough to lease. We moderate abuse, personal info, and solicitation, never opinions. If a community has a real problem, you hear about it first through the themes digest, where it’s fixable intel rather than a public review.

Is this fair-housing compliant?

Every conversation is screened for steering and protected-class language in real time, and residents get a short fair-housing briefing before their first chat. That’s more oversight than informal “ask a current resident” interactions have ever had.

How is this different from our resident referral program?

A referral program pays residents to mine their own network. Worthwhile, but supply-limited: it only reaches people your residents happen to know. ResThreads points the same resident credibility at the traffic you already pay for, so it scales with your marketing instead of your residents’ contact lists. Most communities run both.

What’s in it for residents?

A reward for every move-in their conversations produce. You set the amount, and most communities mirror their existing referral bonus. Recognition matters too: residents who represent their community tend to re-commit to it.

Can management read the chats?

No. Residents are candid because the conversations are private. You receive anonymized themes and full lease attribution instead, which is the part you can act on.
